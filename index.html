Text a Charity

Text a Charity is the first project in my portfolio.

This app allows one to pick a charity one is interested in and from there our application will pinpoint similar charities based on location and output descriptions and contact information about the charity. My team and I created a website, text-messaging, and emailing system where users can gain more knowledge on their selected charity-cause. We created a website where a user can enter in an organization they care about in the search bar, and our page will spit out an organization that you can donate to. Once you have entered in an organization , the page will output a description, email info, and additional details about the charity. In addition, our users can even find out about a charity by sending us a message via text message or even email. Basically how this works is our users will text or email us, and in the message/subject line they will enter in a cause they are passionate about(for example cancer). After that, they will receive a message/email displaying information about the charity.



As far a the platforms used, we used Twilio for building the SMS logic in our web application, Just Giving for the database of charities and the donate links, and SendGrid for emailing charity info.



Try it out for yourself now! We created three different services to allow a user to view a charity of their choice.

    • Visit suefeng.com/battlehack2015 and enter in a cause you are interested in. Our search engine will output similar charities based on your location.
    • Text us a charity keyword at +1(309)929-4615. For example send us a message with a text input saying Susan. Our SMS Text Messaging application will output charities with the keyword Susan around your area. If you interested in the first charity that pops up, text back MORE for more details; or text back NEXT to see the next charity that was found.
    • Email us at me@tac.bymail.in . Send us a charity name on the subject line and our email deliverability function will output your desired charity.
